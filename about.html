About me

Hi! I'm a Somerset, UK based Front-end Web Developer. I love computers, cats and tea. Buy me any one of these three, and I'll be happy as can be.

I've always been interested in some form of technology, over the years I've dabbled in photography and graphic design, in my free time and professionally. While I've always been passionate in a creative field, I didn't really feel I'd found my calling until 2017.

I started teaching myself web development in January. A close friend of mine suggested I learn, and it's a hobby I haven't been able to put down! Juggling freelance sound engineering, performing in a band and working a full time job, I've also been cramming 20+ hours a week of coding since...

I started learning HTML and CSS. And now feel confident using them on a daily basis. I began learning jQuery, which is a whole heap of fun, but I was then directed towards learning JavaScript first, to get a solid foundation of what jQuery is really about. While I'm sure I'm losing hair wrapping my head around it, it really is alot of fun! The possibilites have really opened up since learning even the rudiments of JavaScript.

I used sites such as FreeCodeCamp and Codecadamy to get myself started. However, following a curriculum doesn't always pay off. The structure given and the knowledge needed are sometimes at odds. Ho hum! While I do still use these, I've also started trawling through the many, many inter-tubes, gathering some knowledge, and even more bookmarks...

I have a strict eye for legible code, and I'm always looking for a clean and simple answer. I find taking the time to deeply understand the best practices yields the best results.

At the moment, some of my learning and/ or reference material are MDN, JS For Cats, Node School, Eloquent JavaScript and of course GitHub.

"Code every day. Never stop learning." - Abraham Lincoln, probably

Thanks for reading!
